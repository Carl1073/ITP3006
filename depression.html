Depression

Depression is a widespread and dangerous mood disorder that results in an over feeling of sadness and disengagement.

What Causes Depression?

There are studies shown that quite a number of factors could actually lead up to depression. Depression is frequently brought on by changes in the chemistry of the brain. The fact that depression typically runs in families is another regular feature. Life events or diseases can cause an individual to be depressed. Sometimes, these may never have an external trigger at all.

What are the symptoms of Depression?

  • Feeling sad, anxious, or “empty”
  • Feeling hopeless, worthless, and pessimistic
  • Crying a lot
  • Loss of interest in hobbies and interests that once enjoyed
  • Decreased energy or fatigue
  • Have difficulty in concentrating, remembering something, or making decisions
  • Have difficulty in sleeping, early morning awakening, or oversleeping
  • Appetite or weight changes
  • Thoughts of death, suicide, self-harm, or suicide attempts
