Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorders is also known as "Manic depression". Those who suffer from this kind of mood disorder experience both manic (high) and depressive (low) instances. In manic phase, patient feel a higher mood, more energy, and not sleepy. In depressive phase, however, patient feel fatigue, a no interest in any activities, and a low mood.

What Causes Bipolar Disorders?

Research suggests that there can be many causes which include genetic factors, environmental factors, and their interactions. Genetic factor is a part of this disorder. People with a gene may develop the illness triggered by environmental factors, such as physical illness or other problems in life.

What are the symptoms of bipolar disorders?

The patient may have manic or depressive symptoms depending on which way his mood has swung. The followings are descriptions on the symptoms in the corresponding phase.

    Manic Phase:
  • Feeling euphoric or angry
  • Being overly confident
  • Being energetic
  • Filling with ambitious plans and ideas
    Depressive Phase:
  • Constant sadness
  • Lack of enjoyment of normal activities
  • Feeling of despair and hopeless
  • Always fatigue
