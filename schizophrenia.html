Schizophrenia

What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a chronic, severe mental disorder that affects the way a person thinks, acts, expresses emotions, perceives reality, and relates to others.

The Main Causes of Schizophrenia

The main causes of schizophrenia can be categorized as follows:-

  • Genetics:
    There is often a family history of schizophrenia, indicating a genetic factor. It is more likely to develop if a family member has the disorder.
  • Structural changes in brain:
    Studies show that people with schizophrenia may have structural differences in their brains, including larger ventricles or less gray matter, and these differences can affect cognition and behavior.
  • Previous drug use:
    Some drugs, particularly hallucinogens and stimulants, can induce or worsen symptoms in those who are susceptible.
  • Environmental factors:
    Prenatal exposures to infection, psychosocial stressors, and trauma may play a role in the development of schizophrenia.
