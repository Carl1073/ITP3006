About Us

We offer a trusted environment and inspire people across the world out of a mental health crisis. We hope to promote understanding, build connections, provide help and support and give people the tools to cope, enjoy left and manage their day-to-day living.

Who We Are

We are a group of people who can identify with those challenges of mental health. And no matter whether you have schizophrenia, depression, bipolar disorder, PTSD, OCD or any other condition, you are not alone. Our club members are composed of staff and volunteers that have come a variety of backgorunds, (not just victims) and those in need of a supportive hand.

Our Mission

At Mental Health Concerning Club, we aim to:

  • Raise Awareness: Educate and encourage open conversations that help to reduce the stigma associated with mental health.
  • Provide Support: Create a place free of judgement to express experiences, share resources and give encouragement.
  • Empower Growth: Inspire people to ask for help, practice resilience and take care of themselves.
  • Promote Connection: Create an environment where people feel welcome, appreciated, and heard.

What We Offer

  • Support Groups offer regular online and in-person gatherings for members to share stories, listen, and learn from each other.
  • Educational resources include articles, workshops, and suggestions for managing mental health and comprehending problems.
  • Community actions to reduce stigma and raise mental health awareness.
  • Events include interesting activities which are consisted of mindfulness classes, arts-and-crafts workshops and presentation by guest speaker to promote relaxation and connection.

Why We Exist

Mental health is seen by many people as something less importantant, a personal matter rather than a public concern. So, we consider mental health to be as important as physical health, and no one should go through this journey alone. Together, we can nurture a world where mental health is accepted, recognized, and prioritized.

Join Us

If you are seeking support, encouragement, or information about mental health, we encourage you to contact us. Together, we will break the stigma and create a better future for everyone.
